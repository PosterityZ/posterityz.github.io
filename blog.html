Realization of a long held dream

May 16, 2020

Born and bred in Kitwe's Kapoto compound,having witnessed first hand the low standards of living in the community he grew up in in his formative years, Noah Aaron Mwewa gave birth to an idea of a platform that would contribute towards poverty alleviation in Zambia. What is now Posterity Zambia started off close to a decade ago as Love Just Foundation, a community based organization aimed at raising and sharing resources amongst the most vulnerable in society

Another Youth supported success story

May 15, 2020

Last year, through the stewardship of our founder, Noah Mwewa, K60,000.00 was raised towards the construction of an ablution block for the children of Elim Orphans and Vulnerable Community School of Musonda, a Shanty Compound of the great city of Kitwe. The school had been using one pit latrine for a population of over 700 Children. Our aim for 2020 is to help construct a Modern Community School for the same community that will have tools to teach Agriculture, ICT and Entrepreneurship. Gender inclusiveness in the fight against poverty

May 14, 2020

Gender equality opens doors for entire communities to strengthen their food and nutrition security and to improve their social and economic well-being, Empowering rural women is indeed empowering humanity We at Posterity Zambia aim close the gender gaps which can then provide multiple development dividends, including gender equality for women, food security and poverty reduction, improved climate management and peaceful societies. Through empowerment programs and skills development focused on women in the informal sector, we aim to achieve maximum impact in the fight against poverty

It's everyone's fight, now is the time to get involved

May 10, 2020

In our quest to improve the living standards of those that we are called to serve, We understand that the people directly affected by the problems or issues of poverty in the community have to be active participants in the efforts to fight poverty. This means that the affected people themselves are the major participants of the intervention to fight poverty and must be consulted. Their views need to be taken into consideration, and through facilitation, they become part of the process of addressing and resolving the issues they face. Our methods include creation of organized groups within the community to help many people, rather than only a few individuals. Rather than working with individual persons, it is more effective to facilitate collective and organized actions to help strengthen and empower people in poverty through an organization. This means that it is not enough to provide assistance to individually affected persons alone, but through collective organization, each individual is developed and steps are taken to address their problems and other problems in the future. Together we can win this fight.
